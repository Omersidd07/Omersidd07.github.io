Higher capita leads to better sanitation and more accessible sanitary facilites

A basic defintion of a Sanitary Facility- A designated structure or system intended to provide safe and hygienic conditions for human waste disposal, personal hygiene, and the prevention of disease transmission in countries.

Sanitary facilities are essential to every country, and another indicator of how infant mortality rate can be shown to be more prevalent in African countries than American and European countries.

Limited financial resources and inadequate infrastructure contribute to the disparity in sanitary facilities between American and European countries and African countries.

Although all countries in Africa gained population just as other countries did, over time, there was a lack of increase in the sanitary facilities necessary to keep people healthy and free of diseases such as Malaria, as shown previously.
