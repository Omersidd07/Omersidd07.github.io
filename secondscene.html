Countries with higher GDP's are less burdened with historic diseases

Another factor of infant mortality rates being as high as they are in African countries as opposed to European countries can be seen through Malaria presence today.

Despite being exploited for resources by economically prosperous nations in North America and Europe, African countries continue to face disproportionately high Malaria incidences compared to the rest of the world, where the disease is nearly eradicated.
