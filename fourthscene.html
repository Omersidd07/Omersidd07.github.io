What are the takeaways?

Sub-Saharan African countries tend to have higher infant mortality rates than European and American counries.

The financial and medical underdevelopment in this region can be attributed to over-exploitation throughout history by other countries that intended to colonize and mine the region of its resources.

This allows us to further look into differences between these regions, based on sanitary and financial development over time.

Understanding the variations in infant mortality rates across different regions can help inform public health strategies and aid in building a more equitable and sustainable future for financial and medical well-being for African countries.
